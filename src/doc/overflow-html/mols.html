mols

There is "room" for many different kinds of mols, since it is expected that many different kinds of indivisible components may form complexes, DNA/genes are expected in particular for the study of transcription factor binding to regulatory regions.

But at this point in Moleculizer's development, there are only two kinds of mols:

The most common kind of mol is a "mod-mol," which represents a simple protein that admits user-defined modifications (like phosphorylation or alkylation) at user-specified modification sites.

Another kind of mol is a 'small-mol' which represents a small molecule that participates in binding, such as ATP or a drug molecule. Small mols do not admit modifications, and they automatically have one and only one binding site. The unique binding site on a small-mol has the same name as the small-mol itself.
