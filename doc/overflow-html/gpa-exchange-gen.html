gpa-exchange-gen

The nucleotide exchange reactions model here is simplified by consolidating the unbinding of GDP with the binding of GTP into one step. This simplified form of nucleotide exchange happens only when the G-protein is in a particular enabling complex, which is supposed to change the physical conformation of the G-protein to reduce its affinity for GDP and increase its affinity for GTP. In the alpha pathway, this enabling complex includes at least alpha factor, the trans-membrane receptor Ste2, and the G-alpha component Gpa1. It also includes the G-beta component Ste4 in some of the receptor models we have used at MSI.

Technically, the G-protein in the "enabling" complex need not be restricted to be in the GDP-bound state, and this slightly simplifies the expression of the complex. But reactions are generated only for species of the "enabling" complex in which the G-protein is GDP bound.
